About

One of my best from that year was a Game Maker app that was basically a 2D Minecraft Clone. It included procedural world generation, with trees and ore veins, and basic crafting.

In grade 11, I once again studied ahead and finished the curriculum in 2 months, but then went further and completed most of the Grade 12 curriculum as well before the end of the year. At this point I began acting as a teaching assistant, helping to answer other students questions and assisting them with their projects. My time in grade 11 was spent broadening my knowledge, learning JS, HTML, CSS, PHP, C++, C#, and a little bit of Java. I also worked on several Arduino projects, including a car that detected and followed a line on the ground, and a controller for addressable Leds with custom light patterns and the ability to communicate over Bluetooth. This was also the year I designed a website for my Fathers company.

Having completed the majority of the Grade 12 curriculum the year before, I had a lot more free time during that class. I played around with Unity and Unreal Engine, but when I discovered Python, I fell in love. The formatting made programming faster, and the available libraries were incredibly powerful and useful. I first discovered python when researching Neural Networks. I was mesmerized by the concept and committed to making one of my own. I learned that PyGame and Numpy were two highly valuable libraries in python that would help me make it, so I decided to learn the new language instead of sticking with JS as I normally did before. My time since has been doing what projects I can in my free time to reinforce my skills and understanding.
